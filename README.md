# Learning Outcomes vs Infrastructure Gap Analysis in India

## Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between student learning outcomes and school infrastructure across Indian states and Union Territories using National Achievement Survey (NAS) and UDISE datasets.

The objective was to identify educational disparities, infrastructure gaps, and intervention priorities through a data-driven Power BI dashboard.

---

# Objectives

* Compare learning outcomes with infrastructure availability across states
* Identify states requiring urgent educational intervention
* Analyze learning decline across higher grade levels
* Detect regional educational disparities
* Develop a composite educational performance ranking framework

---

# Methodology

## Composite Infrastructure Score

Infrastructure indicators were normalized and aggregated to create a state-level infrastructure score.

---

## NAS Learning Score

Average NAS learning scores were calculated using subject-wise and class-wise mean performance values.

---

## Composite Educational Ranking

A weighted composite score was developed using:

* 60% NAS Learning Score
* 40% Infrastructure Score

This ranking framework was used to identify top-performing and bottom-performing states.

---

# Dashboard Visualizations

## 1. Scatter Plot — Learning vs Infrastructure

### Compares:

* NAS Learning Score
* Infrastructure Score

### Key Findings:

* Meghalaya emerged as a major outlier with weak infrastructure and learning outcomes
* Punjab and Chandigarh demonstrated strong performance across both dimensions
* Most states clustered within moderate-to-low infrastructure and learning ranges
* Several states showed infrastructure-learning mismatch, indicating governance and pedagogy gaps

---

## 2. Bottom 10 State/UT Rankings

### Key Findings:

* Northeastern states dominated the bottom-performing category
* Meghalaya ranked lowest overall
* Assam, Nagaland, Tripura, and Arunachal Pradesh consistently underperformed

---

## 3. Intervention Category Analysis

States were classified into intervention groups based on combined infrastructure and learning performance.

### Key Findings:

* 3 states required highest-priority systemic intervention
* 9 states required high-priority dual intervention
* 12 out of 36 states/UTs required urgent educational attention

---

## 4. NAS Score by Class

Analyzed learning progression from Class 3 to Class 10.

### Key Findings:

* Learning outcomes consistently declined across higher grade levels
* Class 10 recorded the weakest learning outcomes nationally
* Indicates cumulative learning deficits and weak conceptual retention

---

# Major Insights

## Learning Decline Across Classes

Student learning outcomes progressively decline from primary to secondary education levels.

---

## Infrastructure Alone Does Not Guarantee Learning

Several states with moderate infrastructure still demonstrated weak learning outcomes, indicating:

* Teacher capability gaps
* Pedagogical inefficiencies
* Governance challenges
* Weak classroom engagement

---

## Regional Educational Disparities

Northeastern states were disproportionately represented among lower-performing regions.

---

## Need for Dual Intervention

Many states require simultaneous improvements in:

* Infrastructure availability
* Academic quality and pedagogy

---

## Benchmark States

Punjab and Chandigarh can serve as benchmark models for:

* Educational governance
* Infrastructure utilization
* Learning achievement
